What is loop engineering?

KitFire · July 2026 · 5 min read

Loop engineering is the practice of building AI that runs on a loop instead of waiting for prompts: it does the work, a separate check verifies the result against a real standard, it records what it learned, and it repeats on a schedule — without a person driving each step. In 2026 engineers building the leading AI tools, at companies including Anthropic and Google, named the same shift independently: the skill stopped being writing prompts and became designing the loops that run the AI for you. Where a chatbot answers when you type and an AI tool does one job on command, a loop keeps working on its own and checks itself. That — also called AI system design — is exactly what KitFire builds and runs for the businesses it serves: the loops, the checks, and the schedule, so the AI does the busy work while you run the business.

Prompting vs. loop engineering

For two years, using AI meant sitting at a keyboard typing an instruction, reading the answer, and typing the next one. You were the engine. Every result waited on you. Loop engineering removes you from the middle of it. Instead of adding another instruction, you add another part to a system: a step that does the work, a step that checks it, a memory of what was learned, and a schedule that runs it again. The AI keeps going while you're doing something else — or asleep.

The five moves in every loop

Any loop, in any business, comes down to five moves. Most people only make the first two on purpose:

  • Discovery — figure out what actually needs to happen.
  • Handoff — give the task to the AI worker that does it.
  • Verification — check the result against a real standard, not a gut feeling.
  • Persistence — record what was learned so it isn't lost next time.
  • Scheduling — decide when it runs again, and let it.

The last three are what turn a chatbot you have to babysit into a system that runs itself. They're also the three most businesses never set up — which is why their "AI" still needs a person standing over it.

Why a separate check is the whole game

The single most important part of a good loop is that the AI doing the work is not the same AI that approves it. When one model grades its own homework, it defends what it wrote. So a real loop has a separate checker that measures the result against a written definition of done — the way a manager reviews a staffer's work instead of taking their word for it. No check, no loop; you just have a faster way to produce mistakes. KitFire builds that check into every loop we run, and a human still approves anything that reaches the public.
