Done-for-you AI operations

KitFire · July 2026 · 5 min read

Done-for-you AI operations means someone installs the AI workers your business needs, runs them day to day, checks their work, and reports back — so you get the output without managing the tools. You don't buy software and figure it out. You don't prompt, tune, or babysit anything. The email gets handled, the reviews get answered, the leads get followed up — and a human approves anything that reaches your customers. It's the difference between owning a tool and hiring the operator who runs it.

Why "done-for-you" is the part that matters

Every business has now been sold the same promise: buy this AI tool and save hours. The catch nobody mentions is that a tool is a job. It needs setting up, prompting, correcting, and connecting to the rest of your work — and that job lands on the busiest person in the building, usually the owner. Done-for-you flips it: the labor and the management both leave your desk. You approve the output; you don't run the machine.

What's actually included

  • Install — the right AI workers for the jobs you actually have, built on your business facts, not a generic template.
  • Run — they work day to day: email triage and drafts, Google review responses, quote and lead follow-up, website questions and lead capture, phone answering.
  • Check — output is monitored and corrected; mistakes are caught and logged so they don't repeat.
  • Report — a plain-English summary of what happened, with the misses included.

What it costs

Three packages, real numbers, month-to-month:

  • First AI Worker — one worker installed and run. $2,000 install + $295/month.
  • The AI Office — three to five workers running your busy work. $6,000 install + $995/month.
  • The Whole Department — the AI Office plus the full marketing engine (SEO, weekly content, Google Business Profile). $2,200/month.

For context, one part-time office hire runs $2,000–3,500 a month before you've trained them. Done-for-you AI operations is a fraction of that, and it doesn't clock out.

Is done-for-you AI legit?

It's as legit as the people running it and the proof they'll show you. The honest version has three tells: a human approves public output, every action is logged so you can read what was done, and the provider shows real work — misses included — rather than only wins. KitFire runs roughly 90% of its own operations this way and publishes the receipts. Ask any provider to do the same.
