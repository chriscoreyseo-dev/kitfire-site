Questions owners actually ask

Fair questions. Straight answers.

Do I need to understand AI to work with you?
No. You never touch it. You answer a questionnaire, you approve work before it goes public, and you read a plain-English summary of what happened. That's the whole job description.
Won't AI content sound generic?
Generic AI content comes from generic instructions. Every piece we produce runs on your business file — your services, your customers, your voice, your area — and a human reviews it before it ships. When something misses, you say so once, and it's recorded permanently. That's why month three sounds more like you than month one.
What exactly does the $1,750/month cover?
Ongoing SEO (meta tags, long-tail keywords, weekly tuning), a competitive niche strategy for your industry, a weekly LinkedIn post, blog articles, Google Business Profile upkeep, and the growing knowledge file that makes all of it sharper over time — plus full use of the KitFire skills library. The one thing it doesn't include is the initial website build, which is quoted per project because no two are the same size.
How long does a new website take?
Most builds go from your questionnaire to a click-through preview link — one you can open on your phone and browse like the real site — in a matter of weeks. You approve it before anything goes live; your current site stays untouched until you do.
Is a real person involved, or is it all robots?
Both, on purpose. The AI does the labor — that's why the price is a fraction of an agency retainer. A human directs it, checks it, and signs off on everything before it reaches the public. You always have a person to talk to.
