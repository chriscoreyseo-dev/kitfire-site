How KitFire works: draft, coach, win

KitFire · July 2026 · 4 min read

KitFire works in three steps: we draft the AI workers your business needs, coach them on your business facts, and run them so the work gets done — with a human approving everything that reaches your customers. You answer one short questionnaire. You never touch the tools. You approve public work and read a plain-English summary of what happened. That's the whole loop: draft the lineup, coach the team, win — and show the receipts.

Step 1 — Draft the lineup

You answer a short questionnaire about your business — your services, your customers, your area, the way you talk. From that, KitFire installs the right AI workers for the jobs you actually have: a website assistant that answers questions and captures leads, a review responder, email triage, quote and lead follow-up, phone answering. Not a generic template — workers built on your business facts. Your first worker goes live the day you start.

Step 2 — Coach the team

This is the part that makes it a manager, not a tool. KitFire runs the workers day to day, checks their output, and corrects mistakes — and every correction is logged and remembered. Month three sounds more like your business than month one, because nothing that's been fixed gets un-fixed. You're not prompting anything; the coaching happens for you.

Step 3 — Win, and show the receipts

The work gets done: email handled, reviews answered, leads chased, questions answered. A human approves anything customer-facing before it ships. And you get a plain-English summary of what happened — with the misses included, because a report that only shows wins isn't a report. Every action is written down, so you always know what was done, when, and why.

What you do vs. what KitFire does

  • You: answer one questionnaire, approve public work, read the summary.
  • KitFire: installs, runs, checks, corrects, reports — the whole operation.

That split is the point. You keep the decisions and the relationships; the labor and the management leave your desk.
