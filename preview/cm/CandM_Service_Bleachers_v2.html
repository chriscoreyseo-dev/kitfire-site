Overview

Telescopic Bleachers from C&M.

When a school needs to seat 500, 1,200, or 2,000 spectators on game night and convert the gym back to PE class the next morning, telescopic bleachers are the answer. The Problem

What goes wrong with old bleachers

Failed Winch Motors

Original-spec motors burn out around year 12. Replacement without bleacher swap is straightforward — if you know the model.

Worn Steel Cables

Cable fray and broken strands are the #1 finding on annual inspections. Catch it early, replace cheaply. Miss it, lose the seating.

Dry-rotted Seat Boards

Wood seat planks fail around year 20. Aluminum and high-density plastic seats hold up better but eventually crack at the fasteners.

ADA Non-Compliance

Pre-2010 bleachers usually don't have the wheelchair platforms and aisle ramps modern code requires. We can retrofit most systems.

Frequently Asked

Questions ADs ask about Telescopic Bleachers.

How long does a telescopic bleacher install take?
1 week for 500–1,000 seats. 2 weeks for 1,000–2,000 seats. Power-operated systems with chair-back upper rows add 2–3 days for electrical commissioning. We schedule around school calendars — most installs land in summer break.
Can you retrofit our old bleachers instead of replacing them?
Yes — if the structural understructure is sound. We can update seat planks, winches, cables, safety straps, and add ADA-compliant access without replacing the whole system. Costs 20–30% of a full replacement.
What brands do you install?
Performance Sports Systems is our primary partner for telescopic bleachers, and we offer Clarin folding chairs for chair-back upper rows. We're authorized dealers for both.
Do you handle ADA compliance retrofits?
Yes. Wheelchair platforms, integrated ADA aisles, and access ramps can be added to most existing systems. Often a school's first ADA citation drives the retrofit conversation — we've handled dozens.
