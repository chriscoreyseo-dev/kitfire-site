The Problem

What schools need from mats & volleyball gear

Worn Wrestling Mats

Top-surface vinyl tears and seam separation are unavoidable after 8–10 years of season-long use. Re-bonding and resurfacing extend the life dramatically.

Mat Storage Damage

Improper storage (vertical, leaning) causes the foam core to compress unevenly. Wall-rack storage systems are the fix — we install them.

Failed Volleyball Standards

Floor sleeves rust out, height-adjust mechanisms freeze, padding cracks. Annual service catches it before tournament season.

Wall Pad Replacement

Padding around basketball backboards and behind hoops wears through. Replacement panels match the existing color and size.

Frequently Asked

Questions ADs ask about Mats & Volleyball Equipment.

How long should a wrestling mat last?
10–15 years with proper care — meaning rolled and stored on a mat rack, cleaned after every practice, and inspected annually for seam separation. Vertical leaning storage cuts the life in half.
Can damaged mats be repaired instead of replaced?
Yes. We do re-bonding for tears, surface patching for cuts, and edge re-binding for separation. A repair is 10–20% of the cost of replacement and adds years of service life.
What volleyball standard system is right for our gym?
Floor-sleeve systems are the gold standard for competition gyms — strongest, safest, fastest setup. Free-standing systems are for facilities where the floor can't be drilled. We help you pick based on use frequency and competition level.
Do you install custom logos on wrestling mats?
Yes — Resilite mats can be ordered with full-color school logos, mascots, or conference branding. Adds about 4 weeks to delivery time.
