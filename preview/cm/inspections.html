Once A Year, Every Year

Manufacturers call for annual inspection of overhead and load-bearing gym equipment, and most Michigan districts build it into their safety audit. If you can't show a recent inspection record, you're carrying liability.

Why Inspections Matter

Three reasons every athletic director schedules this.

One is the law. One is liability. One is just common sense.

01

It's the Standard

Equipment manufacturers specify annual inspection for overhead backstops, divider curtains, and hoists. Most Michigan school districts include it in their annual safety audit.

02

It's the Liability

An overhead equipment failure during a game can mean injuries, lawsuits, and personal liability for the AD who skipped the inspection. The annual visit costs less than one hour of legal defense.

03

It's the Money

Catching a worn cable, a stressed weld, or a failing motor early costs hundreds. Replacing the whole system costs tens of thousands. Inspection is the cheapest insurance you'll buy this year.

The 12-Point Inspection

Every inspection. Every system. No shortcuts.

Every Item
Documented & Photographed

Our 12-point checklist covers every load-bearing and moving part — anchors, welds, winches, cables, straps, and controls — to manufacturer specifications. Every item documented. Every defect photographed. Every report signed.

After inspection, we issue a written report, tag the equipment with the inspection date, and file a copy for your district records.
